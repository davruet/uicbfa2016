The spectrum of art is unknowable. The UIC BFA Thesis class begins their fall semester ripe with possibilities, and with every decision a student makes towards the production of their art, the closer they come to being pinned down by their own impetus. And so, here they are, the UIC BFA 2016 class, each pinned in by the immense gestures that have taken, and still are taking, place in their intimate, unique studio corner. The massive spectrum that you will find on view here still remains unknowable however no longer in the abstract, but in the visceral realm of experience. What is art for you? What should art do? What do you look for in a work of art? Why? And furthermore, should Art question or embody the very criteria you bring to it?

From my love of film to a world of social activism. I always had an artistic itch, the urge to create, whether it was drawing, photography or video. As a film buff trying to become a filmmaker I found my passion in photography with the idea of honing my skills in framing and composition. I went from photographing the world around me, such as my family and my neighborhood, to finding a cause I believed in and another group which supports the arts in the neighborhood I grew up in and using my photography skills to document our events and actions. I finally came back to the art side of my interest looking to expand my experiences with different mediums and expanding my artistic language.


I came to UIC looking to expand my creative side and make connections with other artist. I felt that, while still being creative, my work was more utilitarian oriented. My art work (my photography and Photoshop skills) served a specific purpose, documenting the work of the community groups I had become affiliated with and creating flyers for events. At UIC I tried to distance my art work from my social activist side but was always aware that there would be chances for overlapping. UIC has given me the chance to explore other mediums and try other processes that I, most likely, would not have come across. My work has expanded to new media arts, experimental video, painting, and installation work. My work brings into question my habits and my perceived norms of society in an attempt to find the connections between us all and touch on the social and philosophical aspects of our consumer culture by presenting that which is recognizable and twisting it into a place of contemplation, from our interaction with technology, the media we consume, and that which we choose to engage with and/or to ignore.
